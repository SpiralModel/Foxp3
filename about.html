Identical mechanisms govern self-nonself discrimination and regulation of class of immune response

It has been long held that when responding to new antigen immune system makes two, separate decisions. In Decision 1, commonly referred to as a self-nonself discrimination, immune system decides whether to respond or not to new antigen. In Decision 2, referred to as a regulation of class of immune response, immune system decides which class of immune response is most effective when responding to new antigen. Here we present for the first time a novel theoretical framework that predicts that identical principles, based on antigen-specific Foxp3+ regulatory T cell mediated control non-productive T cell “spiral” responses, govern both seemingly unrelated decisions of self-nonself discrimination and regulation of class of immune response
